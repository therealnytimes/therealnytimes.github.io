Scandal, Corruption, and Deceit: Roman Catholic Church Withholds Oldest Biblical Text!

New research finds that the extinction of this flightless bird was completely our fault.

By Adam Johnson, Editor in Chief

  • Dec. 15, 2019

BREAKING

Robert Sallo, an Archeologist funded by Northwestern University, has published a sensational finding indicating corruption and scandal within the Roman Catholic Church.

While on a commissioned trip to the Vatican Archives, Sallo claims to have unearthed a codex, hidden in “plain sight” containing two key elements: first, several fragments of writings believed to be from the world’s first murderer, Cain, and what seems to be a letter From Pope Paul V, who commissioned the creation of the Vatican Archives.

Sallo claims that recent renovation and restructuring of the archive left these sensational texts exposed, which have recently been translated and published for public viewing. However, Sallo declined to comment on the context of the passages that were found. The Pope and other officials of the Vatican were contacted, but denied the existence and legitimacy of these documents.

Despite this, forensic analysis and carbon dating have been extensively conducted. First, the letter from Pope Paul V, which was signed with his official coat of arms, has been conclusively dated to the early 1600s. With regards to the biblical fragments, one parchment has been carbon dated back over one thousand years, making it one of the oldest biblical manuscripts.

Further forensic analysis is being conducted on the remaining fragments, as millions eagerly await to see the results.

DISCLAIMER: The texts below have been translated and adapted for universal reading and comprehension.

FRAGMENTS OF POPE JOHN V’S LETTER (Translated):

Carbon Dated: 1640 CE, Translated: December 2019

My dearest Ignatius,

You are well aware of my recent commission to establish the Archives. I am writing to you with absolute urgency… these texts must be hidden…the public shall not be exposed ... the church ...but the Church must survive.

Ensure that it is placed within the Archives, and that it is never opened for the public…. It shall be for the church fathers only.

May God Bless You,

Camillo

FRAGMENT’S FROM THE “BOOK OF CAIN”

Carbon Dated: 1050 BCE, Translated: December 2019

I dreamed such a horrid dream that my spirit was deeply troubled, and sleep left me for days. There was a white ram grazing in an open field full of green pasture. This ram was grazing next to a shepherd, who carefully removed its wool and began to mold it. From the hands of the shepherd sprung a sheep, equally white and vibrant. Now, the shepherd pressed his hands on the sheep, and it bore yet another sheep. The offspring’s wool was black, the shepherd looked upon it with disdain, and struck it fiercely with his cane, leaving a permanent wound. From the Ram and the sheep came another offspring. This time its fur was white, and just as vibrant as the mothers. The shepherd gazed upon it and was pleased.

In a desperate desire to understand my dream, I entreated my mother to interpret it. She responded as such: “The Lord is our shepherd. You were created with the help of the Lord, while your brother, Abel, was not.” This simple response irked me. If I was created with the help of the Lord, why was I looked upon with disdain? Why was I the Black Sheep?

It took me years to understand the true meaning of this dream and to put the pieces together. After yet another vision, I now understand what it means to be made “with the help of the Lord”. I saw how the Lord and my mother conceived me in the same fields I spend my days. Am I the Lord’s constant reminder of his own sinful actions? Is the lord too made of flesh and bones and was tempted to covet and enter a woman that was not his own? I am the product of this sinful act. This surely was not my fault nor a result of any of my own desires. I am left wondering if my father - the man I consider my father, Adam- knows about this. Is he aware of this act? A constant reminder lingers over my head: My true father is the man my parents praise and pray to and the one they sinned against and betrayed.

Carbon Dated: 970 BCE, Translated: August 2018

After learning the true meaning of my dream, I grew with rage. How was I not the favorite of my mother? Of my father - my real biological father? My entire life made absolutely no sense... for years I have seen Abel get his way in everything we do. When we would kick one another as little children, I was always punished with my father’s walking stick until I was numb. Meanwhile, my mother would hug Abel as he cried and complained. This story repeated itself time and time again. Whenever mother would become sick, I would retrieve water from the well, make a warm broth, and look after her while she slept; however, Abel simply slept and awoke early to work. Abel always worked. He would look after his animals and spend hours brushing their mane.

As I continued to remember all of these moments in which I was belittled while the unloving, disinterested and selfish animal I grew up with got his way, I knew this had to come to an end. I needed to be treated as the first born. But my vision… my vision perturbed me. I saw how I was created, how God knew my mother, came into her, and she begged for a child. I am the son of God, yet he prefers my brother’s offering.

We each worked hard on our own offerings. I spent my days tilling the ground, making sure that each weed was removed, dehydrated I worked day and night to make sure my offering was my best work for my father. But my father neglected my gift, how could he be so irrationale? My brother’s offering of meat and fat, of which he had more than twentyfold what he gave God, was preferred by my father. How could my life be this unfair?

Carbon Dated: 968 BCE, Translated: August 2018

It eventually came time for us to give our offerings to the Lord. Abel offered the fat of his sheep and I gave the best of my fruits. The Lord smelled them both; “surely, he will like the sweet smell of my offering” I thought to myself. The Lord, however, had no regard for my offering; he took Abel’s offering and rejected mine! Abel’s offering was only possible because I allowed his sheep to graze on my land and feast on my crops. While I had to work hours cultivating my own crops, Abel received his sheep from our father. Everything has been handed down to Abel, and now he is receiving the grace of the Lord- my father- too. I had to confront him. After calling for him for hours while he ignored me and feasted on figs, I said “let us go out into the field brother”, and I dragged him and his cattle with me.

“Hearken to me brother, for I am the Firstborn. With my help, your offering was received. Pray for me, so that I may be blessed in the eyes of the Lord” I said.

Abel responded: “Why would I pray for you? You are a false son”

I was furious. How does he know this? Abel was laughing hysterically, as if my conception was a jest. "Silence! Get thee gone!” I screamed. He would not stop, and he continued to ridicule me. As he let out a continuous bellow, he unleashed his cattle and they trampled over my crops. I had to put an end to it. I couldn't stand his constant degradation. I needed space; I needed him out of my way, but he was blocking my path. "Please," I pleaded. But he refused to grant my request.

In a fit of frustration and rage, I pushed him away and he fell. His head struck a rock and he began to bleed. The ground greedily lapped his blood. I cried out to him, yet he did not respond. What have I done? I tore my clothes and as I began to mourn, I saw God on the other end of the field.

He inquired: "Where is your brother Abel?"

This question was perplexing, as Abel was dead next to me. I sarcastically declared, "I do not know; am I my brother’s keeper?"

When his eyes finally found Abel he said, "What have you done? Listen; your brother’s blood is crying out to me from the ground! You will be cursed from the ground, which will never receive your blood. You will be a fugitive and a wanderer on the earth forever"

As the Lord, my Father, continued to rage, I felt a strange burning sensation on my arm. The sensation lingered and the pain became unbearable, and I asked for the Lord to either help or let me die. He responded “You will not die. Whoever kills Cain will suffer a sevenfold death. '' The pain began to subside, yet as I looked down on my arm, I observed a horrid burn. The burn seemed to spell out “א” For Abel. I had been marked, branded as an outcast forever.

Carbon Dated: 100 BCE, Translated: September 2019

After receiving that ghastly mark, I felt the words of my father that “you shall be a fugitive and a wanderer on the earth” ring through my ears. Forever isolated and still unsure of my exact punishment, I settled far from the perfection of Eden in the land of Nod. It was in Nod where I knew my wife Awan and she bore my son Enoch. Awan’s beauty was unparalleled and her eyes radiant with energy, as if she was one of the only women on earth. Together we built the city of Enoch; each day I worked hard to till the fields, harvesting grain, grapes, and olives, as the inhabitants of our city prospered, and the walls continued to expand. I wondered when my punishment would take action and soon began to forget all about my unloving, traitor of a father.

Hidden from the face of my father, I felt liberated. No longer being watched every morning, tested every moment, and scrutinized every second, I truly had free will. No longer forced to waste the harvest which I toiled over on futile offerings, I shared it with my people; no one was left hungry and we feasted, drank, and laughed each night. It was intoxicating. Again, I remembered the words of my father that “whoever kills Cain will suffer a seven-fold death”, and scoffed, thinking “the fool has let me continue in bliss forever”. While those around me aged and declined, I remained full of life and vigor.

All the days Awan lived were three hundred years, and she died. The days of Enoch were three hundred and fifty years, his son Irad two hundred and sixty years, and the son of Irad Mehujael died after one hundred and fifty years. No parent should have to bury their child, let alone their grandchildren, and my soul grew hollower with each passing. Now, the words “Whoever kills Cain will suffer a sevenfold death” tormented and mocked me each day, and I came upon the sobering reality that the curse of Cain is to live. My hatred has burned for my unjust father ever since.

These sensational texts have generated buzz across the nation. Sallo claims to have possession of more hidden texts from Cain and he plans to embark on another expedition soon. As always, we will continue to keep you updated as these bombshell findings unravel!
